About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Toronto, working with Prof. Yong-Baek Kim. My research focuses on quantum spin liquids, particularly dipolar–octupolar quantum spin ice in Ce-based pyrochlores. I specialize in computational methods such as exact diagonalization, gauge mean-field theory, Monte Carlo simulations, and numerical linked-cluster expansions.

Beyond physics, I am deeply interested in high-performance computing, algorithm design, and quantitative finance. I am currently preparing to transition into quantitative research roles, building on my background in large-scale numerical simulations, statistical physics, and advanced coding.

Research Highlights

  • Large-scale exact diagonalization of a 32-site pyrochlore cluster for thermodynamics and dynamics.
  • Gauge mean-field theory of dipolar–octupolar quantum spin ice.
  • Monte Carlo simulations and Landau–Lifshitz dynamics of emergent gauge fields.
  • Disorder-averaged numerical linked-cluster expansion calculations.

Selected Projects

  • HPC Simulation Codes: Custom C++/CUDA frameworks implementing Lanczos, shift-invert, TPQ, and Krylov methods.
  • AI-Powered Web Application: Multi-language platform integrating large language models, LaTeX rendering, and custom Markdown/KaTeX pipelines.
  • Quant Finance Side Project: Machine-learning model to predict arXiv paper impact using citation metrics and Bayesian ensembles.
