About Me

ZZ

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Toronto, working with Prof. Yong-Baek Kim. My research focuses on frustrated spin systems and quantum many-body physics. I specialize in computational methods such as exact diagonalization, Monte Carlo simulations, and numerical linked-cluster expansions.

Frustrated Magnetism Quantum Spin Liquids Exact Diagonalization Monte Carlo Methods Computational Physics

Research Focus

My research investigates the rich physics of frustrated quantum magnets, where competing interactions prevent the system from settling into a simple ordered state. This frustration can lead to exotic quantum phases such as spin liquids, characterized by long-range entanglement and fractional excitations.

I employ state-of-the-art computational techniques to study these systems, including:

  • Exact Diagonalization: Systematic study of small system sizes to understand ground state properties
  • Quantum Monte Carlo: Large-scale simulations to explore finite temperature physics
  • Numerical Linked-Cluster Expansions: Thermodynamic limit calculations for infinite systems
