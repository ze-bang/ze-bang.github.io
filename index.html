About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in theoretical condensed matter physics at the University of Toronto, supervised by Prof. Yong-Baek Kim. My work centers on emergent phenomena in strongly correlated and frustrated quantum materials, where competing interactions give rise to unconventional ground states and excitations.

Methodologically, I integrate analytical field-theoretic approaches with large-scale numerical simulations to extract dynamical response, entanglement structure, and finite-temperature behavior in regimes beyond perturbative control.

Research Focus

I study quantum many-body systems in which frustration, topology, or proximity to fractionalization produces strongly correlated phases that elude simple quasiparticle descriptions. My current interests include dynamical signatures of spin liquids, entanglement diagnostics of competing ordered and disordered phases, and finite-temperature crossovers in low-dimensional magnets.

To address these problems, I employ exact diagonalization, quantum Monte Carlo (where sign-problem free), numerical linked-cluster expansions, and tensor-inspired post-processing of dynamical correlators.
